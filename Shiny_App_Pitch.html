Phone Insurance Sensibility Calculator

Randy Sherwood

The Problem

  1. Many mobile phone carriers offer insurance plans that are of little benefit to most consumers
  2. Some, such as T-Mobile, even include the first month "free" so that you have to remember to opt out later
  3. Not only does the monthly cost add up, but they all have a hidden deductible that come out of the customer's pocket should a phone break
  4. If customers did the math, most would find that these insurance plans are a poor idea

Game Theory

Using a game theory approach, we can determine whether or not buying phone insurance is a good decision based on some characteristics of the plan and cost of the phone.

Breaks k times Never Breaks
With Insurance -cn-dk -cn
Without Insurance -xk 0
  • Let c be the monthly cost of the insurance
  • Let d be the deductible if we were to break the phone
  • Let k be the number of times we break our phone
  • Let x be the cost of replacing the phone without insurance
  • Let n be the number of months we pay for insurance

\[ p(-cn-dk)+(1-p)(-cn) = p(-xk)+(1-p)(0) \]
